# Design Notes

## Foundations

- A quiet, reflective design that feels handwritten and personal  
- A soft, analogue atmosphere inspired by notebooks, practice journals, and pinned instrument notes  
- Minimal ornamentation, allowing each reflection to stand on its own  
- Typography chosen to evoke warmth, informality, and gentle pacing  
- A layout that encourages small pauses rather than long reading sessions  

The foundations of the site are shaped by a desire for calm simplicity, creating a space that feels like a collection of musical fragments rather than a traditional website.

## Navigation

- Navigation remains minimal and unobtrusive, with only essential links shown  
- The homepage acts as a simple gateway into the different instrument groups  
- The About page is intentionally separate, with a clear return path  
- Notes avoid navigation clutter to preserve their quiet, handwritten feel  
- Predictability is prioritised with no hidden menus and no shifting elements  

Navigation should feel light and effortless, offering direction without disturbing the reflective tone of the site.

## Visual Identity

- Soft, neutral backgrounds to keep focus on the handwritten notes  
- Sticky note style cards with subtle shadows and gentle rotation  
- A handwritten font for note bodies to evoke warmth and immediacy  
- Instrument related colours used sparingly, primarily as accents  
- Whitespace used generously to create a sense of breath and stillness  

The visual identity aims to feel like a small desk of instrument reminders, tactile, quiet, and intentionally imperfect.

## Structure and Layout

- Each note follows a simple rhythm: title then handwritten body  
- Instrument pages group notes without overwhelming the reader  
- The homepage presents the instrument groups as a soft grid of entry points  
- Layouts remain consistent across notes to preserve the sense of a curated collection  
- Line lengths and spacing are tuned for short form reading rather than long essays  

The structure is designed to support brief, reflective encounters rather than extended reading sessions.

## Interaction Principles

- Interactions remain subtle with gentle hover shifts and no distracting animations  
- Links are understated but clear, avoiding bright or intrusive colours  
- Cards lift slightly on hover to reinforce the tactile, pinned note metaphor  
- Accessibility remains essential with readable contrast and clear focus states  
- The interface should always feel secondary to the content  

Interactions should feel soft and supportive, enhancing the sense of a quiet musical space.

## Growth and Maintainability

- New notes can be added easily within each instrument group  
- The structure supports quiet expansion without disrupting the tone  
- Notes remain timeless, not dated and not blog like  
- The site grows like a small collection of musical reflections, not a feed
