  The Drum Kit emerged in early 20th century America when separate percussion instruments were brought together so one player could handle the rhythm alone.
  The introduction of the bass‑drum pedal and later the hi‑hat gave drummers new ways to shape time, turning simple patterns into full grooves.
  From jazz swing to rock backbeats and modern electronic‑influenced styles, the kit has remained the core pulse that holds everything together.
