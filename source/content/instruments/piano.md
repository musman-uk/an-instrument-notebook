  The modern Piano came in early 18th century Italy when Bartolomeo Cristofori replaced the plucked mechanism of earlier keyboards with hammers that responded to touch.
  That transition better control over dynamics, making the instrument suitable for both delicate lines and dramatic passages. Across centuries, and even today, 
  it has moved through classical halls, jazz clubs, and film scores, always carrying the same balance of clarity and weight.
