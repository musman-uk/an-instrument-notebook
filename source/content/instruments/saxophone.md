description: >
  The Saxophone came in 19th century Belgium when Adolphe Sax set out to bridge the gap between woodwinds and brass, creating an instrument with a reed’s flexibility and a horn’s projection.
  Its warm, vocal tone later became a defining voice in jazz, carrying everything from soft ballads to sharp, rhythmic lines. In the modern era it can effortlessly move between genres,
  but still holds the same expressive pull that first set it apart.
