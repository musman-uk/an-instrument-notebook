  The Violin took shape in 16th century Italy when makers like Amati and later Stradivari refined earlier bowed instruments into a lighter, more responsive form.
  Its clear tone became central to classical music, carrying both solo lines and ensemble roles with ease. 
  Over time it moved into folk traditions, jazz, and film scores, always keeping that same blend of precision and emotional pull.
