title: "Spanish Classical Guitar"
image: "placeholder"
description: >
  The Spanish classical guitar carries a long line of Iberian craft, but it found its modern shape in the 19th century when Antonio Torres settled its proportions and gave it the voice players still recognise today. His instruments were built for gut strings, later replaced by nylon, which gave the guitar a softer and more intimate sound that responds to the smallest shift in the fingers. It became a central part of Spanish classical music and the pulse of flamenco, shaped by musicians who learned its language slowly, through repetition and memory. Today it is still chosen by people who want an instrument that answers to a gentle touch more than anything else, something that reveals its character only when you sit with it long enough to hear what it is trying to say.
