## Origins  
The modern classical guitar emerged in late‑1800s Spain, shaped by the work of Antonio Torres Jurado, whose designs established the proportions and bracing patterns still used today.

## Cultural Roots  
Its lineage reaches back to the vihuela and guitarra latina of Renaissance Spain. Over time, it became central to Spanish classical music, flamenco, and intimate solo performance traditions.

## Character  
Warm, rounded, and expressive, the classical guitar speaks in softer colours – ideal for lyrical melodies, intricate fingerstyle, and emotional nuance.
