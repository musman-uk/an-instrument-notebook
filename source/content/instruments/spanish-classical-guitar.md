  Preceded by Iberian craft, the Spanish Classical Guitar emerged in the 19th century when Antonio Torres built it with gut strings, later replaced by nylon, that gave the guitar a softer and brighter sound and became a central part of Spanish classical music and the pulse of flamenco. Today it featues across many cultures, and is something that reveals more of its beauty the longer you spend with it.
