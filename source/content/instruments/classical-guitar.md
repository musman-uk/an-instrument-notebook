  Preceded by Moorish Influence and Iberian craft, the modern Classical Guitar emerged in the 19th century Spain when Antonio Torres built it with gut strings, that gave the guitar a softer and brighter sound and became a central part of Spanish classical music and the pulse of flamenco. Today it is more commonly constructed with nylon strings and features across many genres, with its beauty still evergreen.
