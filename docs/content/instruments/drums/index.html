🥁 Drums

Drums are among the oldest instruments we have. They appear in every culture, shaped from whatever materials people had at hand. Wood, skin, metal, clay — each one creates a different voice, a different kind of pulse.

Some drums speak in deep, grounded tones. Others cut through the air with sharp, bright strikes. Together they form patterns that guide movement, dance, and ceremony. Even a single drum can hold a conversation between rhythm and silence.

This page is a quiet recognition of how universal drums are and how they continue to connect people through shared rhythm.
